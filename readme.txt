Introduction:

World War II (1939-1945) had major social, economic, and environmental impacts worldwide. 
This project analyzes data from 1925–1960 to examine changes in life expectancy, 
Gross National Income (GNI), and CO₂ emissions before, during, and after the war.
It also explores how countries such as Germany and Japan recovered from the war’s effects
and how industrial growth influenced economic development, quality of life, and environmental change over time.



Problem Statement:

This project analyzes historical data on Gross National Income (GNI) per capita, life expectancy,
 and CO₂ emissions during and after World War II. 
Covering the period from 1925 to 1960, the study examines how the war influenced economic conditions,
 environmental indicators, and human quality of life, with particular focus on industrialized countries such as Germany and Japan.
The project also investigates patterns of decline and recovery associated with wartime destruction and post-war reconstruction.
 By analyzing these historical trends, the study aims to provide a clearer understanding of the long-term economic,
 social, and environmental impacts of large-scale global conflict.



Objectives:

To analyze historical trends in Gross National Income (GNI) per capita, life expectancy, and CO₂ emissions between 1925 and 1960. 
To examine the impact of World War II on economic conditions, environmental indicators, and human quality of life. 
To compare patterns of decline and recovery in industrialized countries such as Germany and Japan during and after the war period.
 To identify how major historical events influenced industrial activity, public health, and economic growth over time. 
To visualize historical trends using data analysis and graphical representations in Python. 
To raise awareness of the long-term social, economic, and environmental consequences of global conflicts.
